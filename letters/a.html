Dictionary: A

Ablative

adjective
  1. (in some inflected languages) noting a case that has among its functions the
    indication of place from which or, as in Latin, place in which, manner, means, instrument, or agent.
noun
  1. the ablative case. Abbreviation: abl.
  2. a word in that case, as Troiā in Latin Aenēas Troiā vēnit, “Aeneas came from Troy.”
